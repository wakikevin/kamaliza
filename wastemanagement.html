The youth of Kamaliza were among the first in Nairobi to pioneer door-to-door garbage collection. To this day the business provides income for local youth and keeps the community clean and rid of waste. By reducing garbage-borne diseases, the service is critical to the welfare of residents who pay a monthly fee to have the waste removed.


In 2009 Kamaliza constructed a recycling centre to process solid plastic waste and this enterprise has increased the value of Kamalizaâ€™s waste management business. The centre employs up to six youth at a time who collect and process solid waste that is then sold in pellet form to a variety of industries.


The centre has not only improved the communities overall sanitation, it has saved tonnes of solid waste from entering landfills. Although Kamaliza continues to be the forefront of innovation in the recycling sector, we are always reaching out and searching for opportunities to increase production.
